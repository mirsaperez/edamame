Dinner Recipe 3/16/2021

Slow-Roasted Leg of Lamb

1.Place the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, rosemary leaves and mint in a food processor and blitz until well blended. Cover the meat with the marinade, working it in to the flesh well, and leave the lamb to marinate for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator.

2.Pre-heat the oven to 160°C/140°C Fan/Gas Mark 3. Put the marinated lamb in to the roaster and season with salt and pepper. Cover the roaster with foil and place into the oven to roast for 3 hours.

3.Remove from the oven and take off the foil. Cut the garlic heads in half, horizontally, to expose the cloves. Arrange the garlic around the meat and spoon some of the meat juices over. Roast, uncovered, for another 2 hours until the meat is tender. Scatter the apricots in the roaster around the meat and heat through.

4.Fry the limes on a Toughened Non-Stick grill and then place in the roaster. Scatter pomegranate seeds over the meat and serve with hummus, cucumber with yoghurt, olives, sun-dried tomatoes and fresh radishes.

5.In a food processor, combine the tahini and lemon juice. Process for 1 minute, scrape the sides and bottom down, then process for 30 seconds more. Add the chickpeas and blitz, then add boiling water and process until smooth. Season with salt and pepper and spoon into a mixing bowl.

6.Cover with cling film and refrigerate. Just before serving, spoon the hummus into serving bowls with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of paprika on top.

7.Remove the ends of the cucumber, then cut it in half lengthways and scrape out the seeds. Slice and place into a bowl.

8.Mix the yoghurt, mint and garlic in a mixing bowl and season with salt and pepper. Spoon the yoghurt over the sliced cucumber and mix well.

9.Make your hummus the day before serving; the taste will be better the next day.
